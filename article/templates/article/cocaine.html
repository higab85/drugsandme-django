Cocaine

Coke, Charlie, Snow

{% endblock %} {% block intro-content %}

Cocaine is a stimulant that gives you a quick euphoric feeling. It is extracted from the leaves of a cocoa plant and these leaves have been chewed or drank as tea for centuries in South America. Nowadays, cocaine comes either in the form of white powder or as a ‘crack’ rock. The intensity of effects and potential for abuse vary greatly depending on the form of cocaine you take.

It is the second most consumed recreational drug worldwide, with cannabis being the first. However, without proper care and responsibility, cocaine can be highly addictive and dangerous due to the intense and short lived induced euphoria.

{% endblock %} {% block intro-content-1 %}

Avoid alcohol

Avoid drinking alcohol when doing cocaine. The combination leads to the production of a toxic chemical, cocaethylene, that is extremely harmful to your liver.

{% endblock %} {% block intro-content-2 %}

Check the purity

Check the purity of your cocaine before using it. Cocaine off the streets rarely comes in its purest form, it is frequently cut with other chemicals and drugs to increase its weight.

{% endblock %} {% block intro-content-3 %}

Know your limits

Cocaine can make you feel over confident and sometimes even invincible, it can cloud your judgement and lead you to make reckless decisions that you may regret later.

    How? How much? When? For how long?

    Read our section on dosing and tolerance in ME for more information.

    As with any drug, the correct dose for you depends on factors such as weight, gender, metabolism, whether you have taken the drug recently or not, amongst many others.

    The amount of cocaine you should take and the time it takes for the effects to kick in depends greatly on the purity and cutting agents mixed in. We highly advise you to test your cocaine before using it. The advice provided below are based on the effects of pure cocaine. However, we understand that it is extremely difficult to find pure cocaine, so start off small and see how you feel.

    How you take it matters...

    Powdered Cocaine

    • Snorting is the most common way of using it. People usually divide the powder out into lines with a card and snort it with a straw or a piece of paper (maybe diagram with a card and lines). Snorting through one nostril over a long time can lead to nasal ulcers or damage the septum.

    • Gumming is another popular way of doing it. A small amount is applied on the upper and lower inside of the lips and the gum. Your lips and gums might feel numb from the cocaine. Gumming can damage your gums and lips.

    Crack cocaine

    • Smoked is the only way to use crack cocaine. It is usually smoked using a crack pipe. The effects of it will be felt immediately (10- 20 seconds) after smoking. However, it is much more potent and addictive than powder cocaine. It can also cause respiratory problems such as asthma and emphysema.

    Source: Nutt, 2012

    How much?

    The purity of cocaine varies significantly from place to place and dealer to dealer. The cocaine that you picked up from your dealer last month might be completely different from the one you just picked up. So, start off small and increase the dose gradually.

    When do the effect kick in and for how long?

    The ‘START’ time below is when you will usually begin to feel the effects of the cocaine from the time when you first take it. The ‘DURATION’ is roughly the length of time you will experience the effects, after which the effects will start to wind off and you might start to feel the calm down effects.

    Snorting and gumming cocaine:

    • START: 1-3 minutes
    • DURATION: 30 minutes

    Smoking crack cocaine:

    • START:15 seconds
    • DURATION:15 minutes

    Head over to our ME section if you would like to know more about harm reduction.

    There are certain precautions you should take before doing cocaine. The advice below helps you to be physically and mentally prepared before doing it. We want you to enjoy your experience as much as possible, but just in case you have a bad experience or some of the unwanted side effects, we have provided information on how to take care of yourself when you are in full swing. Finally, there are those uncomfortable or undesirable effects after the high have worn out, we will provide you with some practical tips on how to have a better calm down and help you to reduce the harm done to your body and brain.

    {% endblock %} {% block harm-reduction-stage-1 %}

    • It is difficult to obtaining a 100% pure cocaine, therefore we highly advise you to test your drug before taking it. Powdered Cocaine is frequently cut with other white powder substances, such as adulterants and speed, to increase its volume and weight. It is extremely difficult to know what these other drugs and impurities might be, so for your own safety and wellbeing, do try to test the purity of your cocaine before using it. Here is also a list of the common cutting agents found in cocaine.

    • Part of reducing the harm to your body is maintaining good hygiene. Before snorting the cocaine, divide it on a clean surface and snort it with an unused straw; try not to use a note as you never know what’s on it.

    • Injecting cocaine can cause damage to the skin and veins, which might lead to sores.. Sharing injecting equipment risks infections such as HIV, Hepatitis B and C. It is also the most addictive way of taking cocaine.

    • If you have any pre-existing heart conditions or pregnant, we strongly advise you to stay away from cocaine. It increases the heart rate significantly and can cause problems for the foetus.

    {% endblock %} {% block harm-reduction-stage-2 %}

    • Avoid mixing cocaine with other drugs, especially alcohol. Cocaine makes you sober and this perception can lead to excessive binges as you don’t seem to get any drunker. More importantly, cocaine and alcohol react to form, cocaethylene (link to wiki or something), a much more harmful and toxic substance. Just remember not to drink too much when you are on cocaine.

    • Cocaine can make you take more risks than you usually would be with. It can also cloud your judgement and lead you to make irrational decisions. Try not to do it alone, it would be even better if you do it with someone more experienced.

    • Taking cocaine in excessive amounts can cause heart attacks, seizures or death. In the case that you overdose, do not doubt to call the ambulance service, you will not get into any trouble. Here are some of the symptoms that people show:

      • Disorganised behaviour
      • Fear
      • Paranoia, suspiciousness
      • Haluucinations
      • Irritability and aggressiveness, often panic attacks
      • Some people can become extremely antisociable
      • In extreme cases, stroke, seizures, pulmonary oedema, aorta rupture heart attack or death can occur

    • Snorting only through one nostril can cause bleeding and build up. Try alternating between the two nostrils and washing them with water.

    {% endblock %} {% block harm-reduction-stage-3 %}

    • Cocaine can make you feel on top of the world and one of the reasons people becoming addicted to it, is the temptation to do it over and over again once the effect has worn off. It has also been shown that frequent cocaine use dramatically reduces the blood reaching the brain and may lead to brain damage or dysfunction. Like any other drug, we advise you not to binge and keep your cocaine consumption for special occasion.

    • You might find it hard to fall asleep after taking cocaine. It also suppresses your appetite, so try to drink plenty of water the next day and have a health and nutritious breakfast, loaded with proteins fibres and carbohydrates, even if you are not hungry.

    • Never take more cocaine to overcome the hangover!

    Dependence

    Cocaine is one of the most addictive recreational drugs (graph). While chewing coca leaf hardly generates addiction, smoked cocaine is one of the most addictive drug habits. This is explained by the speed it reaches the brain and the time the high lasts.

    Even though cocaine is classified as highly addictive it does not mean you will get hooked with the first trial. 18% of users of cocaine have been seen to become addicted to it. However, limit its consumption for very special occasion and never introduce it in your daily lifestyle.

    Please read ME for more general information about recreational drugs.

    Snorting is the most common way of consuming it. After chewing coca leaf, it is the least addictive way contrary to what many people think. Crack users are more prone to get into habitual use and addiction. Injecting solved cocaine in water is the most harmful way to do it.

    FAQS

    People can get addicted with just one try

    This is not true for any drug. Addiction is a complex disorder that it is still not fully understood. It relies on many different factors such as the times a particular drug is consumed, the quantity used, the family history of mental health, social interactions.
